Equipment Guide

Installation

The image above is a guide for installation. In order to install the Articulating Manipulator to the VideoRay Pro 4, place the center float on top of the BlueView Skid Bottom Plate or the Pro 4 Skid Centre Plate, seen here. Place the Articulating Manipulator over the center float. Position the Articulating Manipulator so that it can be screwed into the center float using the 4X M4X40 and M4X25 SHCS screws. If additional information is needed on VideoRay Pro 4 parts, please see the pro 4 equipment guide.

Inventory

1.Skid Plate

Pro 4 Skid Centre Plate or Blueview Skid Bottom Plate- The centre plate attaches to the float.

2A.VideoRay Center Float

Articulating Manipulator Standard Float- Attaches to the Articulating Manipulator body and connects the body to the skid.

2B.Blueview Center Float

Blueview Center- The Blueview Center Float attaches to the articulating Manipulator the same way that the standard does. With the BlueView Float and no ballast, the ROV can handle a maximum of 1.75 lbs of extra weight. If the BlueView Sonar is attached it takes up all weight capacity. If the sonar is attached, there is no room for any other accessories.

3.Articulating Manipulator Body

Body- The Articulating Manipulator Body is what connects the jaws to the center float and skid plate. The Body has a ballast at the end that can be removed at will. It may be in the user's interest to remove the ballast if using accessories that would go over the Blueview Center Float's weight limit. The VideoRay Center Float should not be equipped with the ballast.

4A.M4 x 0.7 25 mm Pan Head Screws

Screws- used to hold the body, skid, center float, and center plate to the ROV.

4B.M4 x 0.7 60 mm Pan Head Screws

Screws- used to hold the body, skid, center float, and center plate to the ROV.

5.VideoRay Skid

Skid- Attaches to the VideoRay Pro 4 ROV, connecting the Articulating Manipulator.

Standard vs. BlueView Center Float

With just the standard float and no ballast, the ROV can handle a maximum of 1.14 lbs of extra weight. With the BlueView float and no ballast, the ROV can handle a maximum of 1.75 lbs of extra weight. The BlueView sonar takes up all the extra weight capacity here, so if you have the sonar attached, there is no room for any other accessories. If heavy accessories are equipped to the Pro 4 the BlueView Float should be used and the rear ballast should be detached. If the standard is used with heavy accessories it is possible that the ROV could sink.
