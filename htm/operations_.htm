Operations Guide

The Articulating Manipulator is operated by the use of the Hand Controller. The Hand Controller dictates the movements of the Articulating Manipulator with a two button open and close method of operation.

*Installation - For aid with installing hardware and software, please take a look at the software and equipment guides.

Jaws - All 5 jaws operate similarly with the controller opening and closing the head.

The Parallel Jaw should be used when picking up heavier objects. Be careful of the jaw's closing power, it is possible to inflict harm if not used properly.

The Sampler Jaw should be used when dirt or water samples need to be taken. The sampler has a water tight seal so water samples from particular places or depths can be found.

The Cutter Jaw is used when there are objects that need to be sheared or cut away.The Cutter Jaw is very sharp and should be used with caution. A disntinction between the Cutter Jaw and the other 4, is that it's controls are inverted. When operating the Hand Controller it will work opposite of the other jaws.

The Trident Jaw is capable of grasping circular objects that might elude the Parallel Jaw.

The V-Jaw is slightly wider than the Parallel Jaw and can grasp larger objects, although it is slightly weaker.

For more information on the Articulating Manipulator Jaws see the Equipment and Specifications guides.

Tactics

An almost limitless number of specialized actions can be performed with the Articulating Manipulator thanks to the multi functionality of it's 5 heads. Some tactics to try with the manipulator are:

Retrieving heavy objects from the Parallel Jaw that would normally exceed the maximum weight by way of pulling on the retrieving cable.

Using a jaw and the rotation feature to turn a valve or bolt.
